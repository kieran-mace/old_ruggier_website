Lisa, Paul, James and Blake Taylor

The Taylors are all very well and life in Plett continues to be peachy. Paul continues with Cornerstone Ceilings. He has a good amount of work and the 2nd half of the year looks hectic. So thank The Lord, he is busy and well. Since the split with his partner last October and moving his office to home, I must say I have a brand new happy husband. You know the old one that I married. He has time for the kids which he absolutely loves. We all have lunch together every day and dinner too. Our family life is of paramount importance to him. We have sold our house and move in February 2008. We’re all very sad as we love our beautiful place. But Paul will be building us another one next year and we are very excited about that. The kids are great. James is growing taller each day. He loves school and is looking forward to “big” school next year. He has become a fast runner and has to shoot everywhere like a rocket. He also has a huge appreciation for music. Even in our church which is rather loud and partyish, I have to find ways to restrain him from dancing the building down. He is also learning to appreciate the marine life here. Last week we were on the beach at the end of the day when this school of dolphin arrived to surf the waves right in front of us. They were in a playful mood and did a few flips for us. When that stopped we decided to stand really close to the water (the dolphins couldn’t have been more that 10m away) and we tried to ask them to do more for us. James was squeaking and Blake was blaaa…ing with hands cupped to his face. The next thing, they started again. Jumping and summer-saulting. It was the most rewarding few minutes. James says to me “Mommy, I didn’t know I could speak Dolphin!” He has joined “Nippers” (junior Life Saving) and is having a blast. Paul, Blake and I also have a good time watching him on Friday afternoons which leads onto a time of socializing with others over a few drinks and a braai. Blake is growing fast and has absolutely NO fear. I call him my thumper. He jumps off couches and walls as if he’s going onto a trampoline. He is a very happy child and is forever smiling. He has just started to say Mama, and Dada. The name James is a bit hard for him, so “Das” has to suffice. As for me, my life is good. My work is fabulous. I love selling property. I have been alone in my office for nearly 2 years now and finally have found someone to join me. Lanice started with me in August. It is going so well. We even open in prayer in the mornings. Our plan is to take this small agency to another level in the not too distant future.
